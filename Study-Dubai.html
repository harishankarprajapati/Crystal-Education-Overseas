About Dubai

Dubai is the most popular city in the United Arab Emirates. Dubai is known for its Supremacy in Technology and innovation. Dubai is one of the most popular travel destinations in the world. It is a truly cosmopolitan city, attracting students from all around the world. 

Dubai International Academic City (DIAC): This purpose-built free trade zone hosts globally recognized universities, training centers, e-learning facilities, and research companies. Many of these were previously part of the Dubai Knowledge Village, another dedicated education zone.

Medium of Instruction: Public universities in Dubai primarily use Arabic as the medium of instruction, with an emphasis on English as a second language. Private universities, on the other hand, predominantly teach in English, catering to expatriate communities.

Accreditation and Quality Assurance: The Ministry of Higher Education and Scientific Research (MOHESR) oversees higher education in Dubai. The Commission for Academic Accreditation (CAA) licenses institutions and accredits degree programs. Additionally, the Knowledge and Human Development Authority (KHDA) ensures quality assurance.

Equivalence Checks: The KHDA checks for equivalence between degree programs in Dubai and those taught elsewhere. This ensures that Dubai graduates have internationally recognized accreditation if they wish to work abroad after graduation.

Popular Programs: Dubai offers a diverse range of academic programs, including world-class business programs, engineering, design, media studies, and education. MBA programs are particularly popular among students. Employment Opportunities: Upon completing their studies, students can explore various job prospects in Dubai. In-demand fields include accountancy, engineering, teaching, and hospitality. The city’s dynamic economy welcomes skilled professionals from diverse backgrounds.

Visa Categories: Dubai provides different work visa options, including theGreen Visa, Standard Work Visa, Golden Visa. These pathways facilitate employment and long-term residency for qualified individuals.

Affordability: When comparing currency conversion rates, the Dirham (AED) tends to be more favorable for Indian students than USD, CAD, or GBP.

Affordability is a significant advantage for those planning to study in Dubai.

Convenient Travel: Dubai’s strategic location allows for easy travel between India and the UAE. A mere three-hour flight connects New Delhi to Dubai, enabling students to visit their families during special occasions without hassle.

Cost Of Studying & Living In Dubai

The average tuition fee for undergraduate programs for international students vary depending on the institution and location. On average, pay between AED 37,500 and 70,000 annually. For postgraduate international students, the average is between AED 55,000 and 75,000 per annum.

The average living cost in Dubai is around AED 4,255 per month excluding tuition fees. However, the exact expenses are subject to vary from one university to another and also one region to another. The exact cost to study in Dubai can only be determined once you shortlist universities.

Weather condition:-

1. Summer (April to October)

  • Extremely hot weather with temperatures regularly exceeding 38°C (100°F).
  • High humidity, making the heat oppressive.
  • Frequent dust storms due to windy conditions.
  • Thunderstorms, tropical cyclones, and dust storms are possible.
  • Overnight lows around 26°C (79°F).

2. Winter (October to April)

  • Milder temperatures with an average daytime high of around 22°C (72°F) in January.
  • Strong thunderstorms can occur, accompanied by lower temperatures.
  • Urban heat island effect keeps Central Dubai slightly warmer.

Lowest recorded temperature: 5°C (41°F).
